Changing Volume

Possible Breating Variations

Breathing Process

Radially arranged struts are covered with an elastic material and joined at the upper and lower ends to form a bundle of struts. An artificial muscle pulls the two bundles soundlessly towards each other, thereby increasing the volume by arching the struts outside.

Dual-chamber concept

The breathing chamber is located in a rigid outer shell, creating two separate spaces. During the breathing motion, the volume of the chambers changes inversely. The dual-chamber concept halves the required space of the overall system.

Room Airflow-Simulation

Simulation of the air movement in the room to ensure that the air cleaner incorporates all the room air into the circuit.
